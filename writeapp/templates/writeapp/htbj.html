Whoami?

I am an experienced network engineer and a Family man with a 6 yrs old daughter at time of writing.

My OSCP journey started around 1st quarter of 2020.

Why I did it?

I have been hearing about this field since I started my IT career but never really thought of getting into it.

I thought that this was only for those who started young and have extreme coding skills.

Then after 5 years of doing same things everyday (networking), I got bored and wanted a challenge.

The thought of entering the field of penetration testing came to mind so I asked my friends who are already in the field and all of them have common advise ,

"If you want to get in, you must do the OSCP" .

Thus my journey begins.

As others have done, my main platform of choice for preparing for OSCP is Hack The Box.

I got the VIP subscription so I can practice with retired boxes and read some walkthroughs.

This has really helped me a lot as I have no prior hacking experience.

Reading other's walkthrough and watching IPPsec's videoes has boosted my skills and confidence .

I followed the TJnulls list of OSCP like boxes and other easy/medium boxes for the first 2 months on a daily basis.

Once I got familiar with the flow, I started including live boxes on the mix and if I am stuck, I rely on forums to get going.

I also do python studying during this time and to avoid burnout, I alternate between HTB and python learning.

Fast forward, after 5 months of doign HTB, I managed to get 66 box rooted and elevated my status from n00b to pro-hacker.

I finally stopped procrastinating and paid for the 30 days OSCP lab time.

This is where it got serious. I have a regular 9 hours work, so I allocated time 2 hours before and 2 hours after my shift to atleast do 1 boxes everyday in the lab.

I tried reading the PDF materials , but most of it have already been learned during pre PWK learning so I skipped it.

Some machines in the Lab are more difficult than the ones I encountered in HTB and vice-versa.

Was able to pawn 37 boxes in the Lab and scheduled my exam 1 week after the lab expiration.

My 1st attempt Dec 29 2020 7 am. (FAILED)

I literally cried as I have invested so much effort and was planning on nailing this 1st time.

I got 67.5 pts. BoF , 20 pts, 10 pts user on 25 and nothing on the other 20.

I hit the 67.5 point mark at the 12th hour, so I got overconfident that I only need to priv Esc on the 25

or get a low-priv user on the other 20 to get another 10 pts to pass..

I started writing my report at this point , priv escalation is where I am confident and I always thought

that once I get a user, then it is good as complete.

Man I was so wrong, I tried for hours until the very last minute of the exam and was not able to priv esc.

I switched from the 25 pt box and the other 20 at random phases during the remaining time hoping to get another

10 pts and this throwed me out of my game.

I also didn't sleep and was taking only little breaks like 5 mins every 2 hours which is not really good.

I turned emo during 1st week of failing the exam and had doubts whether I have to push or just go back to my

network career. With the support of my wife and daughter, I got back and scheduled again for another attempt.

Before the exam, I did a few HTB round to refresh and watched random youtube videos explaining

specific attack vectors. I want to avoid burn out before the 2nd attempt so rather than prepping for technical , I prepped myself mentally.

Watched some K-dramas, played video games and such.

I reminded myself that this is not about learning a specific technology to pass, it is all about the methodology.

The methodology acquired from IPPsec videos and HTB from previous months has been already established.

I need to start fresh. Expect the best and prepare for the worst.

2nd attempt January 29 2020 9 am. (Passed!)

I learned from my previous attempt and took 10-15 mins break every hour .

I also didn't waste too much time on reporting and organizing my report until I know I got the required pts to pass.

I took screenshots along the way and just dumped them on Word and CherryTree.

Luckily for me, I got a repeat of my 25 pt box which I had trouble doing priv esc before.

This saved me time on doing the user part.

In short, I was able to get enough pts to pass this time.

BoF(25) + 25 + 20 = 70 pts.

Was not able to get foothold on the other 20 pointer .

Once I got the 70 pts mark, I started gathering my notes. I did not want to fail due to missing screenshot as I read

stories of poeople pawning all the boxes and failing due to incomplete report.

I took a nap at the 18th hour for 3 hours. Then the last 3 hours of the exam, I tried again pawning the

remaining 20 and 10 pt box . But no luck til the last minute.

Reporting

I got so excited knowing that I have enough points and even though I lack sleep, I tried to complete my report

and submitted it right away after the exam time ended. Then I slept for good 6 hours and when I woke up, I reviewed my report and

noticed that I have a major typo. Instead of putting System Vuln 192.168.3.x , I placed 192.168.2.x on one of the machine.

I was so paranoid that I might fail due to this , because I only have exact 70 pts. I prayed that offsec will consider

given that I have complete screenshot.

Sunday 3 p.m , 30 hours after submitting my report. I got the email from offsec.

The first line gave me chills when I read " We are happy to inform you".

I didn't finish reading and Immediately called my wife to share the good news!

My take and advice to others preparing for the OSCP.

  1. Hackthebox or similar platform is a must before enrolling in OSCP.
    • The materials Offsec provided in the course is awesome but it is not enough to pass the exam.
    • IPPSEC rocks! I mean this , 80% of what I have learned is from watching IPPsec videos on retired HTB boxes. https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCa6eh7gCkpPo5XXUDfygQQA
    • I love reading writeups from 0xdf as he does good explanation beyond root on why and how the vulnerability works. https://0xdf.gitlab.io/
  2. This is a doable exam even though you have 0 hacking experience.
    • Like many others, all you have to do is invest the time and with the right mindset, you too can be a part of the OSCP family.
  3. You can't memorize all the commands. Take good notes!
    • I am sure there are people like "Mike Ross" that can remember everything he wants to remember.

      • But for ordinary people like myself, I have problems memorizing.

      The key here is having good methodology. If you know what to search for, there are tons of notes available in the internet.

Other tools used during exam:

SecureCRT - This is a good terminal as it lets you configure macros and buttons. (I am lazy typing in typing, i just create a 1 button call if I want to fire up my smb or http)

CherryTree - Note taking program, dump all your commands /notes/screenshot here.

NotePad ++ - I like the dual view of notepad ++ and auto syntax highlighting for python. This also has other cool features like macros and compare.
